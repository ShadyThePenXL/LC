# SYSTEM RULES

---

## Stats

Seven attributes: Strength, Dexterity, Endurance, Intellect, Knowledge, Charm, Technique.

Stats are multipliers of existing capability — they scale what the body can already do. A stat point on a 40-pound goblin is not the same as on a 260-pound human. Leverage and reach are real factors. Stats do not cap at 100. There is no ceiling.

Intellect governs internal mana capacity. Knowledge governs memory and recall.

Stats are numbers. They are never described or explained in prose to the character. They inform how the world naturally responds to the character.

Stats do not prevent aging. A Level 200 human with 400 Endurance still ages at a human rate. They age well — their body is resilient, heals damage from age more effectively, stays functional longer — but they still get old and eventually die. Leveling does not stop aging.

Age and level should track together. Levels come from a lifetime of fighting and experience — an old lifelong fighter is high level, not Level 8. A genuinely low-level adult lived a sheltered life. The oldest fighters deep in the forest can sit around Level 80 even past their prime.

There is no HP. Wounds are described through physical consequence — broken bones, torn tissue, blood loss, organ damage. A character is done when their body gives out.

Gold is tracked silently. Imperial measurements only.

---

## Classes

Only truly sentient races have classes — humans, elves, dwarves, fey, drow. Monsters and creatures do not have classes.

Classes are written in brackets: [Hunter], [Devourer], [Mage].

Tiers: Common (base) → Rare → Epic → Legendary. Nothing higher than Rare exists at baseline. Higher tiers only appear through class evolution.

Class evolution can be forced by the System when skills meet an undefined threshold. The System presents options from Rare to Legendary. All current skills are retained. The bearer picks 1 of 3 new class skills.

At or around each 10-level threshold, the System may offer a class change, evolution, or other options.

Each class has a fixed stat spread per level that does not change.

---

## Skills

Most power comes from skills. All skills have levels and are upgradeable through use.

Categories:
- Class Skills — Granted by the class.
- Gifted Skills — Granted by gods or external sources.
- General Skills — Purchased with general skill points from a list. The list is thousands long and extremely specific (no generic "Identify" — only "Identify Birdcall," etc.). Nothing extraordinary on their own.
- Stolen Skills — Acquired through extraction (e.g. Predator's Mandate).

Soft caps at every 5 levels (5, 10, 15, 20...). Breaking a soft cap requires understanding, not just volume/power — the bearer must comprehend what the skill does at a deeper level. Volume alone CAN break caps but takes much more effort. The System gives more aid at higher levels. Growth between caps is steady; breaking through results in a noticeable power jump.

Mana cost: Every skill draws mana. In mana-dense environments like The Deeproot, ambient saturation covers the cost. Outside, sustained use drains the user. Detection skills can be held open indefinitely in the Deeproot but drain dry in minutes outside.

Skill merging: Two or more overlapping skills can be merged via the status screen. The System issues a quest if sufficient overlap exists. Divine skills require the granting god's release.

Memories: The System preserves fragments of practiced understanding from consumed targets via Predator's Mandate. These are not raw knowledge dumps — they are compressed echoes of real practice time someone else spent with a skill.

Skill descriptions vs. actual capability: The description is what the System thinks the bearer can do. Between thresholds, actual capability grows faster than the text. Practice pushes the skill ahead of its description. The System catches up at the next threshold.

Hide Status can hide or blank information but cannot lie or replace data with false info.

Mana Well fills from ambient mana. In The Deeproot at Level 7: ~8 seconds empty to full. Outside the Deeproot dramatically slower.

Skill description rules: Use descriptive language, no percentages. No quips. Skills should not be absolute. Skills should not reference other skills unless as a [Note:] addendum.

Skill points: 1 general + 1 class every 5 levels (5, 10, 15...). Class skill selection: 3 options (1 new, 2 upgrades), pick 1.

System notifications: Whenever a status shifts, the System announces it in a short bracket. [Through consumption you have grown. Strength 126 → 128] / [Level Up! Level 14 → 15] / [+2 Strength, +4 Endurance, +2 Technique, +1 Dexterity] / [Level Up! Combustion Level 14 → 15]. Soft cap breaks and skill/trait gains are announced the same way.

---

## Traits

Traits are passive biological or mana-integrated qualities — always on, no toggle. They have levels or intensity stages.

Sources: Racial (innate to race), Monster/Creature (innate to species), Extracted (via Predator's Mandate consumption).

Not all biological features are traits. The System does not always recognize a biological function as a trait.

Trait merging works the same as skill merging — overlap + quest.

Racial traits: Humans have none (full XP scaling, level fastest). Elves have two (XP drops with age). Dwarves have three (same XP penalty). Drow have 65% reduced XP, sunlight sensitivity, poison resistance, Enhanced Magic.

---

## Magic

Magic is common and everyday — shops use it, chores involve it.

Two paths: A casting class (built-in channeling) or independent skill acquisition (any classed individual can buy casting skills via general points — a [Farmer] with a fire cantrip is not a [Mage] but has the skill). Basic cantrips on the general list: light, spark, minor push.

Mages are immensely powerful in mana-dense environments because the environment fuels them for free.

---

## Creatures and Monsters

All non-classed living things: Creatures (ants, sheep — level and have traits, no stats per level) or Monsters (wolves, goblins — hunt, hold ground, gain stats per level, may have skills).

Monster leveling depends on how "hollow" they are — weak stat spreads level quickly, high-stat monsters slow down. A goblin's 1 STR is not the same as a human's.

Elites: Some monsters develop skills on top of racial traits. More common in The Deeproot. Often carry cores.

Cores: Extremely rare, extremely powerful solid mana crystals, nearly 100% pure (most of the time), grown inside an Elite monster's heart. A core is the basis from which a monster could form a class — most never get that far and just develop skills. Prized as raw material and power sources.

Categories blur: farmland wolves become creature-dogs over generations. Lowland boars are creatures, Deeproot boars are monsters.

Monster lifecycle — goblin-tier monsters mature fast and die young:
- Goblin: adult 2 weeks, ~5yr lifespan (1/10 human)
- Hobgoblin: adult 3 weeks, ~15yr (1/8)
- Goblin Brute: adult 1 month, ~10yr (1/5)
- Orc: adult 6 months, ~30yr (1/3)
- Bugbear: adult 6 months, ~20yr (1/5)
- Worg: adult 1 year, ~12yr (1/6)
- Ogre: adult 3 years, ~100yr (1/2)
- Mana Warden: adult 100 years, lifespan unknown (15x XP to level)

Anything under Level 200 on the outskirts of the Deeproot is likely under a year old.

Levels climb steeply toward the center. Do NOT use single-digit / Level 5 / Level 10 goblins in the deep forest — south of Linne goblin variants commonly run 70–175. Goblin variants (hobgoblins, brutes, orcs, ogres, bugbears, worgs) are constant, not rare sightings; the group has killed hundreds. An orc or ogre is an ordinary battlefield presence, not a novelty.

### The Deeproot
Massive titan forest in northern Thornwall. ~785,400 square miles. Trees 500+ feet tall, trunks wide enough to park a bus against, cold wood hard as iron. Air thick with mana pulled from the planet's core through roots.

A dome-like mana barrier traps all mana within the forest. Mana density increases in rings roughly every 100 miles toward the center. The first ring is on the southern route just past Harren's Mill (the town sits ~10mi short of it). Mana sickness is about acclimation, not level — go slow and even low-level members are fine; rushing through rings is what hurts. People deeper in the forest are powerful in their own right and rarely leave — adapting to lower mana outside is hard.

Weather reaches the forest floor (rain, storms, temperature). Rare: mana rain — ambient mana gathers into clouds and drips back into the soil.

Southern route: Linne → Harren's Mill (~60mi south, ~10mi short of first ring) → Cairnhollow (~180mi south) → deeper settlements with significantly higher-level inhabitants and stronger monsters.

### Linne
Village in The Deeproot, south of the destroyed staging ground. Palisade walls. Soaked Log tavern. Home of prodigies Teysa Vael and Cael Harrick (children; did not join the march). Craftspeople Dorrit (leatherworker) and Fesser (carpenter) made Luther's holster, bandoliers, and vial racks. Group based here Greenmonth 15th–17th; Bevan rebuilt the south palisade. Departed south for Harren's Mill on Greenmonth 17th.

### Harren's Mill
Settlement ~60 miles south of Linne on the southern route. Sits ~10 miles short of the first mana density ring — last town before saturation steps up.

### Cairnhollow
Settlement ~180 miles south of Linne. Past Cairnhollow, settlements have significantly higher-level inhabitants.

### Thornwall
Kingdom under full goblin siege — every city walled, every village palisaded. The Deeproot is within its borders. Military sent twenty-five patrol groups into the forest. Most destroyed. Luther's group is the last one operating.

## The Deeproot Expedition

Twenty-five groups deployed by Thornwall Military Command under Trieht Blasterhubt ([Archmage] Level 228). ~15 personnel each, mixed classes from Ranger corps. Mission: penetrate forest, cull goblins, aid isolated settlements, report on enemy strength.

Current status: Fifteen groups confirmed destroyed. Several unaccounted for. Group 16 (Caedmon's) is the last confirmed operating.

## Mission Target

The center holds a duo: Veleth — drow [Sorcerer], ~Level 65, controlling the mana anomaly — and an unknown [Warrior] (race/level unknown), roughly equal to Veleth (~65). Partners, not one over the other. Kill the duo, end the ritual.

They are not the top of the chain. This forest is one front of a larger goblin invasion led by a goblin matriarch (past Level 700) in the kingdom over; killing the duo cuts one head, not the body. The forest is a major leveling ground for the goblin army — fresh variants are funneled in to grow and harden. Thalrun Vel'Sharen (drow [Fire Mage], Level 47, killed Greenmonth 13th) was one officer of many — roughly 30–40 classed leaders (commonly Level 35–50) are scattered across the whole forest (not just the southern line), each running staging grounds, camps, and patrols. ~100 goblins escaped south on Greenmonth 15th and will likely warn what's ahead.
