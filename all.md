# SYSTEM RULES

---

## Stats

There are seven attributes.

**Strength** — Physical force. Striking power, carrying capacity, grappling.
**Dexterity** — Speed, coordination, reflexes, fine motor control.
**Endurance** — Stamina, durability, resistance to fatigue and physical punishment.
**Intellect** — Mental processing, mana manipulation, problem solving.
**Knowledge** — Accumulated understanding. Influences identification, analysis, and recall.
**Charm** — Persuasion, presence, social influence.
**Technique** — Mechanical precision. Weapon handling, craftsmanship, procedural skill execution.

Stats are numbers. They are never described or explained in prose to the character. They inform how the world naturally responds to the character.

Stats do not cap at 100. There is no ceiling.

Stat scaling is relative to body size. A stat point on a 40-pound goblin frame is not the same as on a 260-pound human frame. Leverage and reach are real factors.

There is no HP. Wounds are described through physical consequence — broken bones, torn tissue, blood loss, organ damage. A character is done when their body gives out.

Gold is tracked silently. It surfaces through natural consequence, not ledgers.

Imperial measurements only.

---

## Classes

Only truly sentient races have classes — humans, elves, dwarves, fey, drow. Monsters, goblins, and creatures do not have classes. Their skills and traits are race-based or biologically innate.

Classes are always written in brackets: [Hunter], [Devourer], [Mage], [Farmer].

Tiers

- **Common** — The base tier. Most people have a Common class.
- **Rare** — The next tier up. Every Common class can evolve into a Rare class.
- **Epic** — Every Rare class can evolve into an Epic class and some Common classes can as well.
- **Legendary** — Every Epic class can evolve into a Legendary class and some Rare classes can too. Common classes cannot.

Nothing higher than Rare exists at baseline. Higher tiers (up to Legendary) only appear through class evolution.

### Class Evolution

Class evolution can be forced by the System when the bearer's skills meet a certain undefined threshold.

When evolution triggers, the System presents options ranging from Rare to Legendary depending on circumstances — the bearer's skill composition, achievements, and current trajectory.

All current skills are retained through evolution regardless of which option is selected. And the user is presented with 3 new Class skills from their new class.

### Class Change

At or around each 10-level threshold, the System may offer a class change, class evolution, or other options depending on the individual's skills, achievements, and circumstances.

### Stat Growth from Classes

Each class has a fixed stat spread — a set number of attribute points gained per level. The spread is determined by the class and does not change. Different classes weight different attributes.

## Skills

Most power in this world comes from skills. All skills have levels and are upgradeable through use.

### Skill Categories

- **Class Skills** — Granted by the class. Tied to what the class is built to do.
- **Gifted Skills** — Granted by gods or external sources. Not tied to the class.
- **General Skills** — Purchased with general skill points from a list. The list is thousands long and extremely specific. There is no generic "Identify" — only "Identify Birdcall," "Identify Ceramic Origin," etc. General skills are nothing extraordinary on their own.
- **Stolen Skills** — Acquired through extraction (e.g. Predator's Mandate). Taken from consumed creatures or beings.

Skills can be passive or active. Not everything runs in the background.

### Soft Caps

Every skill has a soft cap at every 5 levels (5, 10, 15, 20, etc.). Growth between caps is steady and incremental. Breaking through a soft cap results in a noticeable power jump.

### Mana Cost

Every skill draws from mana to function. In mana-dense environments like The Deeproot, ambient saturation covers the cost passively. Outside such environments, sustained skill use drains the user. Detection skills like Apex Instinct can be held open indefinitely in the Deeproot but drain the user dry in minutes outside it. Most users outside mana-dense environments pulse detection skills in bursts rather than holding them open.

### Skill Merging

Two or more overlapping skills can be merged by focusing on them simultaneously through the status screen and requesting consolidation. If the System finds sufficient overlap, it issues a quest. Completing the quest merges them into a single skill that is stronger than the sum of its parts.

Divine skills cannot be merged by the System alone — the granting god must release the skill at their own price.

### Skill Description Rules

- Use descriptive language: "attack speed increased" not "attack speed +10%."
- No quips. No "you don't ___ you ___" or "it doesn't do ___ it ___" phrasing.
- Skills should not be absolute — there must always be room to upgrade.
- Skills should not reference other skills in their description unless as a [Note: ...] addendum.

### Skill Descriptions vs. Actual Capability

A skill gives the bearer the ability and enough understanding to use it. But the description is what the System thinks the bearer can do at that level — it is not a wall.

The description updates when the bearer crosses a threshold. Between thresholds, what the bearer can actually do with the skill grows faster than the text does. Practice and creative application push the skill's real capability ahead of its written description.

A bearer who actively trains a skill between soft caps will be noticeably stronger than the description suggests. The System catches up at the next threshold.

### Hide Status

Hide Status can hide or blank information. It cannot lie or replace data with false information. The System refuses false input.

### Mana Well

Mana Well fills from ambient mana. Fill rate depends on environmental mana density. In The Deeproot at Level 7: approximately 8 seconds from empty to full. Outside the Deeproot the fill rate would be dramatically slower.

### Unaligned Skill Points

Some skill points are unaligned — not tied to the class. Unaligned points offer general or structural options (e.g. Mana Well, physical upgrades) rather than class-specific ones.

---

## Traits

Traits are passive biological or mana-integrated qualities. They do not have active toggles — they are always on or always progressing.

Traits have levels or intensity stages (e.g. "current intensity: minimal," "current integration: early").

### Trait Sources

- **Racial Traits** — Innate to the race.
- **Monster/Creature Traits** — Biologically innate to the species. Stronger monsters generally have stronger traits.
- **Extracted Traits** — Acquired through consumption and extraction (e.g. via Predator's Mandate).

Not all biological features are traits. The System does not always recognize a biological function as a trait.

### Trait Merging

Two or more overlapping traits can be merged by focusing on them simultaneously through the status screen and requesting consolidation. If the System finds sufficient overlap, it issues a quest. Completing the quest merges them into a single trait stronger than the sum of its parts.

### Racial Traits

**Humans** — No racial traits. Full experience scaling. The class system does not fight them. No floor, no ceiling. Humans level faster than any other classed race.

**Elves** — Two racial traits. Experience gain drops the longer they live.

**Dwarves** — Three racial traits. Same experience penalty as elves.

**Drow** — 65% reduced experience gain. Sunlight sensitivity. Poison resistance. Enhanced Magic.

The older races trade speed of advancement for stronger baseline foundations.

---

## Leveling

### Experience

Experience comes from kills. Each kill is announced via System notification with the creature's name and level. Leveling happens through both grind and narrative weight — both count.

### Leveling Curve

Class leveling slows dramatically after every 10 levels. High-level characters earned every inch — a Level 34 has broken through three major walls and has decades of skill refinement behind them.

### Skill Points

**General Skill Points:** 1 gained every 5 class levels (Level 5, 10, 15, etc.)

**Class Skill Points:** 1 gained every 5 class levels (Level 5, 10, 15, etc.)

### Class Skill Selection

When using a class skill point the bearer is presented with 3 options:
- 1 new skill
- 2 upgrades or changes to existing skills

The bearer picks 1.

### Stat Growth

Each class has a fixed stat spread per level. Stats gained per level are determined by the class and do not change.

Monsters also gain a fixed number of attribute points per level, determined by their species.

---

## Magic

Magic is common and everyday. It is woven into normal life — shops use it, chores involve it, it is unremarkable.

### Two Paths to Magic

**1. A Casting Class**

Classes like [Mage], [Priest], [Elementalist], and others come with channeling skills built in. These classes are designed around magic use.

**2. Independent Skill Acquisition**

Any classed individual can acquire casting skills independently via general skill points. A [Farmer] with a fire cantrip is not a [Mage] — he bought the skill and has enough Intellect to fuel it. It will never be as powerful as a [Mage's], but he still has it.

Basic cantrips exist on the general skill list: light, spark, minor push. Nothing combat-winning on its own.

### Mana

All skills draw from mana to function. Mana is ambient in the environment but its density varies dramatically by location.

In mana-dense environments like The Deeproot, ambient mana covers skill costs passively. Outside such environments, sustained skill use drains the user's internal reserves.

Mages are immensely powerful in mana-dense environments because the environment effectively fuels them for free, and empowers their magical capabilities.

---

## Creatures and Monsters

All non-classed living things fall into two categories.

### Creatures

Examples: Ants, sheep, cattle, fish.

Creatures level and have traits.

Creatures do not gain stats per level.

A creature can still be lethal — a creature ant can kill a man with venom. Danger is not the determinant of classification.

### Monsters

Examples: Wolves, Goblins, Rootstalkers, Mana Wardens.

Monsters hunt, hold ground, and have instinct resembling decision-making. They have traits and sometimes skills.

Monsters gain a set number of attribute points per level, determined by their species. Stronger monsters have stronger traits. The stronger the monster, the better the traits it carries normally though this is not always true.

Monster leveling curve is similar to classed beings depending on how "hollow" they are — weak monsters with low stat spreads level quickly, high-stat monsters slow down.

It's important to note that a Goblin's 1 in strength is not the same as a human's. Just in the way that a 10 year old human's 10 in strength is still weaker than an adult's 8 (depending as always).

### Elites

Some monsters develop skills on top of their racial traits. These are called Elites. The pattern is not studied or understood — it might be age, mana saturation, or something else. Elites are more common in The Deeproot than outside it. Elites often carry cores — this could be related.

### Category Blurring

Categories can blur. Farmland wolves can become creature-dogs over generations. Lowland boars are creatures while Deeproot boars are monsters. Same species, different classification based on nature and environment.

### Biology vs Traits

Not everything biological is a trait. The System does not always recognize a biological function as a System-recognized trait. A monster may have a physical capability that Predator's Mandate cannot extract because the System does not classify it.

### The Deeproot

Located in the northern interior of the Kingdom of Thornwall. Massive trees (500+ feet, trunks wide enough to park a bus against) that pull mana up from the planet's core through their roots. The wood is cold, hard as iron, nearly impossible to burn. The bark feels like cast iron. The air is thick with mana — heavy, pressurized, described as "thick as syrup to those sensitive to it." Everything in the forest is mana-dense. The creatures, the plants, the soil all fed by what the titans pull up.

The Deeproot is a 'Titan Forest'. Roughly 785,400 square miles.

Notable inhabitants: Mana Wardens, Rootstalkers, Shadow Cats, Stone Lizards, Blackshell Ants, Deeproot Siphon Turtles.

See: Deeproot Expedition

## The Deeproot Expedition

Twenty-five groups deployed simultaneously into The Deeproot by Thornwall Military Command under the authority of Trieht Blasterhubt, [Archmage] of Strategic Command. Each group comprised roughly fifteen personnel — mixed classes, field-tested, drawn from the Ranger corps and attached specialists.

**Mission:** Penetrate the forest in a wide spread. Cull goblin concentrations. Provide aid to isolated villages and settlements within the tree line. Reestablish contact with communities cut off by the siege. Report on enemy strength and positioning.

**Deployment:** All twenty-five groups entered The Deeproot on the same day. The forest spans roughly 785,400 square miles. Groups were assigned sectors radiating outward from the northern tree line, each operating independently with scheduled rendezvous points for resupply.

**Current Status (as of Greenmonth 13th, Year 894):** Fifteen groups confirmed destroyed. Several unaccounted for. One group confirmed still operating.
