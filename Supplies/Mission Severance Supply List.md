NOTES ON SUPPLIES
Mithril Steel Alloy — normally reserved for commanders. Expensive. The fact that a full patrol's worth of Mithril weapons was sent signals the severity of the mission. Hollis Ferrow — Crown-licensed armorer, Thornwall Garrison. Makes gear for royals. His smith's mark on patrol equipment is extraordinary. Chimera Leather — denser than enchanted steel at half the weight. The cuirasses are significantly better than standard-issue armor. Whisperweave Cloaks — archmage-treated. Most soldiers never receive these. Ten of them for a four-person team is excess by design. High-Grade Healing Potions — extremely expensive. Will close any wound and replenish all fatigue and blood loss within an hour. Will not regrow limbs (that requires master-grade). Caedmon has never seen one in person. Stimulant Tea — functions similarly to a powerful stimulant. Highly addictive. Should be manageable for high-stat individuals but still dangerous with prolonged use. Edgekeen Arrows — fragile. Likely one shot each. Handle with extreme care. Ward-Killer Sword — untested. The Crown forged it blind based on the Crown Magus's theoretical recommendations. Nobody knows if it works against the actual target. Anti-Mage Countermeasures — all theoretical. The Crown has no confirmed intelligence on the drow [Mage]'s capabilities. Dispel Chalk works on shaman-level wards. Higher-order wards may resist or trigger on contact. Scrying Burns — common, easily produced, spent freely. The one normal consumable in the kit. Water Purification Stones — may be empowered inside the Deeproot due to ambient mana. Testing needed. Supply Quantity — everything is provisioned for ten personnel. The group is four. This more than doubles effective supply duration. The overall quality of supplies led Toller to observe that nobody sends gear this good to a team they expect to survive. The supplies are an investment in the mission, not the people.
