Greenmonth 16th.

### Glass Munitions

~6:02 AM — Luther realized glass solves the core engineering problem. A physical shell eliminates mana bleed, mana merging, and shell degradation; glass fragmentation replaces mana sub-munitions entirely.

6:19 AM — Luther sprinted to [[Linne]] (~4 minutes), pulled [[Wyn Drace]] from his bunk, carried him back to camp over his shoulder. Wyn set up a glass workstation — surfaces, tool racks, prototype shelves — in under a minute with [[Summon Glass]].

6:26 AM → 3:00 PM — Luther and Wyn iterated on glass munitions.

The core problem: mana primed to detonate on air contact can't sit stable in a sealed vacuum (it cools and settles), and mana stable enough to store won't detonate on exposure. Solution: a **dual-layer charge** — a hot compressed combustion core surrounded by a cooler mana buffer. The buffer insulates the core in storage; on glass break, air ignites the buffer first and the cascade reaches the core.

Working glass grenade achieved by noon — crosshatch-scored inner surface for controlled fragmentation, airless seal, dual-layer charge. First test run 9/11 clean; after Wyn adjusted for seal defects and buffer thickness, the next 8 were 8/8.

[[Combustion]] leveled 14 → 15 (soft cap reached at 15). New capability: layered combustion charges of differing intensity may be constructed within a single assembly, maintaining separation until triggered.

**Claymore variant** — half-dome, flat-backed, tempered glass rear plate (Wyn's innovation; blast focuses forward without the back shattering), scored curved front face, ~60-degree fragment cone.

[[Wyn Drace]] — [[Summon Glass]] reached Level 20 and broke its soft cap; selected the variant glass-types upgrade (tempered, laminated, crystalline), with foundational knowledge of each granted by the System. [[Shape Glass]] 21 → 23. [[Heat Protection]] 13 → 14.

Crystalline glass became the standard for munitions — harder, denser, thinner walls, better structural integrity. Enabled miniaturized land-mine payloads: belt-pouch vials (small — enough to remove a limb) and satchel vials (large — enough to remove a body), designed to pair with [[Bevan Roe|Bevan]]'s [[Trap Craft]] triggers.

By 3:00 PM: 12 standard glass grenades, 4 claymore charges, 8 frag grenades, 11 land-mine payloads (6 small, 5 large). 8 failures discarded.

### Glass Firearms

~8:05 PM — Luther drew a Glock 19 diagram. Wyn built a crystalline-glass pistol: 8-inch rifled barrel (6 spiral grooves), chamber, frame, grip. No moving parts, no firing mechanism — combustion is the propellant. First test with pure mana projectiles failed (no mass for the rifling to grip; mana dispersed in flight). Luther designed a glass-projectile feed; Wyn built a mana-driven magazine (a passive mana current replaces the spring). 112 tempered-shell crystalline-core rounds produced (12 magazine + 100 spare) — open-backed hollow chambers the user fills with charge and caps with combustion.

Pistol test: 12 rounds at 30 feet into a titan tree, all impacted. Cratered bark, glass embedded. Dinner-plate grouping at 30 ft (no sights, point-and-fire). Rifling stabilized the rounds. Functional but limited.

Luther drew a Desert Eagle / .50 AE diagram. Wyn built the larger variant: 10-inch barrel, wider bore, deeper and more aggressive rifling, heavier frame, thicker chamber walls, 8-round magazine. Rounds: double-walled crystalline tip (penetration) over a single-wall body (fragments after entry). 28 rounds produced (8 + 20 spare).

Desert Eagle test: one round at 30 feet into a titan tree — fist-sized entry hole, 3+ inches deep into wood that turns steel blades; the round penetrated intact, then fragmented inside the wound channel.

Luther commissioned a leather hip holster from Dorrit (leatherworker, [[Linne]]) — done by morning — plus bandoliers (padded, wool-insert, 6 grenades each) and vial racks from Fesser (carpenter, [[Linne]]) — slotted hardwood, latched lid, leather-lined, 11 payloads per rack.

Luther practiced freeform grenades the rest of the day and into the next morning. [[Mana Well]] leveled 7 → 8.
