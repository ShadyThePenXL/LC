## Magic

Magic is common and everyday. It is woven into normal life — shops use it, chores involve it, it is unremarkable.

### Two Paths to Magic

**1. A Casting Class**

Classes like [Mage], [Priest], [Elementalist], and others come with channeling skills built in. These classes are designed around magic use.

**2. Independent Skill Acquisition**

Any classed individual can acquire casting skills independently via general skill points. A [Farmer] with a fire cantrip is not a [Mage] — he bought the skill and has enough Intellect to fuel it. It will never be as powerful as a [Mage's], but he still has it.

Basic cantrips exist on the general skill list: light, spark, minor push. Nothing combat-winning on its own.

### Mana

All skills draw from mana to function. Mana is ambient in the environment but its density varies dramatically by location.

In mana-dense environments like [[The Deeproot]], ambient mana covers skill costs passively. Outside such environments, sustained skill use drains the user's internal reserves.

Mages are immensely powerful in mana-dense environments because the environment effectively fuels them for free, and empowers their magical capabitlies .

### Mana Types Are Distinct

Mana is not one uniform substance. Ambient mana (loose in the environment), the mana a skill draws and produces, the mana bound up in a being's levels, and the internal reserve tied to Intellect are all different from one another and do not freely interchange.

This is why a [Devourer] cannot simply "detonate" the mana inside someone's body or burn away their levels — that mana is a different kind, bound and structured, not loose fuel to be ignited. Luther does not know this. He assumes mana is mana.

### Mana Shaping

Mana can be shaped. Casting classes teach this innately — the magic skill itself carries the knowledge of how to take hold of mana and direct it. Every mage can shape mana; how well depends on the individual's training, skill, and talent.

In its base form mana does nothing on its own. It only becomes flame, water, force, or any effect when something acts on it and gives it guidance. Left alone, mana would flicker into random effects moment to moment — turning to flame or water or whatever at random every second. It needs a hand on it to do anything coherent. **Shaping is the act of taking control of mana and holding it to a purpose.**

A consequence: a shaper cannot make their mana burn — or do anything — without maintaining control over it. The control *is* the effect. Let go and it stops.

**Luther's shaping:** He has decent control thanks to the [[Combustion]] Memory from [[Thalrun Vel'Sharen]]. His effective shaping range is about 30 feet with full control. From 30 toward 60 feet he loses a lot of precision; past 60 it degrades further; past roughly 80 feet his control is next to nothing.
