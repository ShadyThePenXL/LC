## Stats

There are seven attributes.

**Strength** — Physical force. Striking power, carrying capacity, grappling.
**Dexterity** — Speed, coordination, reflexes, fine motor control.
**Endurance** — Stamina, durability, resistance to fatigue and physical punishment.
**Intellect** — Mental processing, mana manipulation, problem solving. Also determines baseline internal mana capacity — all classed beings have an internal mana reserve that scales with Intellect.
**Knowledge** — Accumulated understanding. Influences identification, analysis, and recall. Also governs memory — ability to retain and recall information scales with Knowledge.
**Charm** — Persuasion, presence, social influence.
**Technique** — Mechanical precision. Weapon handling, craftsmanship, procedural skill execution.

Stats are numbers. They are never described or explained in prose to the character. They inform how the world naturally responds to the character.

### Stat Scaling

Stats are multipliers — they multiply what the bearer currently has. A point of Strength on someone who already trains and fights multiplies existing capability. Stats do not cap at 100. There is no ceiling.

Stat scaling is relative to body size. A stat point on a 40-pound goblin frame is not the same as on a 260-pound human frame. Leverage and reach are real factors.

### Aging

Stats do not prevent aging. High stats slow degradation and keep the body in peak condition longer, but time still passes. Elves and dwarves live longer due to racial biology, not stats. Leveling does not stop aging.

Age and level should make sense together. Levels are earned through kills, conflict, and lived experience — an old person who has fought and worked their whole life should be a high level, not a low one. A genuinely low-level adult is someone who has lived a sheltered, sedentary life. An eighty-year-old veteran is not Level 8; an eighty-year-old who never left a quiet village might be. The oldest, most experienced fighters deep in the forest can sit around Level 80 even past their physical prime — their level reflects a lifetime of fighting, their failing body reflects their age.

### General Rules

There is no HP. Wounds are described through physical consequence — broken bones, torn tissue, blood loss, organ damage. A character is done when their body gives out.

Gold is tracked silently. It surfaces through natural consequence, not ledgers.

Imperial measurements only.
