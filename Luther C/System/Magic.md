## Magic

Magic is common and everyday. It is woven into normal life — shops use it, chores involve it, it is unremarkable.

### Two Paths to Magic

**1. A Casting Class**

Classes like [Mage], [Priest], [Elementalist], and others come with channeling skills built in. These classes are designed around magic use.

**2. Independent Skill Acquisition**

Any classed individual can acquire casting skills independently via general skill points. A [Farmer] with a fire cantrip is not a [Mage] — he bought the skill and has enough Intellect to fuel it.

Basic cantrips exist on the general skill list: light, spark, minor push. Nothing combat-winning on its own.

### Mana

All skills draw from mana to function. Mana is ambient in the environment but its density varies dramatically by location.

In mana-dense environments like [[The Deeproot]], ambient mana covers skill costs passively. Outside such environments, sustained skill use drains the user's internal reserves.

Mages are immensely powerful in mana-dense environments because the environment effectively fuels them for free.
