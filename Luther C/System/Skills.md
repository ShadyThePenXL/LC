## Skills

Most power in this world comes from skills. All skills have levels and are upgradeable through use.

### Skill Categories

- **Class Skills** — Granted by the class framework. Tied to what the class is built to do.
- **Gifted Skills** — Granted by gods or external sources. Not tied to the class.
- **General Skills** — Purchased with general skill points from a list. The list is thousands long and extremely specific. There is no generic "Identify" — only "Identify Birdcall," "Identify Ceramic Origin," etc. General skills are nothing extraordinary on their own.
- **Stolen Skills** — Acquired through extraction (e.g. [[Predator's Mandate]]). Taken from consumed creatures or beings.

Skills can be passive or active. Not everything runs in the background.

### Soft Caps

Every skill has a soft cap at every 5 levels (5, 10, 15, 20, etc.). Growth between caps is steady and incremental. Breaking through a soft cap results in a noticeable power jump.

### Mana Cost

Every skill draws from mana to function. In mana-dense environments like [[The Deeproot]], ambient saturation covers the cost passively. Outside such environments, sustained skill use drains the user. Detection skills like [[Apex Instinct]] can be held open indefinitely in the Deeproot but drain the user dry in minutes outside it. Most users outside mana-dense environments pulse detection skills in bursts rather than holding them open.

### Skill Merging

Two or more overlapping skills can be merged by focusing on them simultaneously through the status screen and requesting consolidation. If the System finds sufficient overlap, it issues a quest. Completing the quest merges them into a single skill that is stronger than the sum of its parts.

Divine skills cannot be merged by the System alone — the granting god must release the skill at their own price.

### Skill Description Rules

- Use descriptive language: "attack speed increased" not "attack speed +10%."
- No quips. No "you don't ___ you ___" or "it doesn't do ___ it ___" phrasing.
- Skills should not be absolute — there must always be room to upgrade.
- Skills should not reference other skills in their description unless as a [Note: ...] addendum.

### Predator's Claim / Mandate Specifics

[[Predator's Claim]] fires on full consumption of a creature. Can be directed toward specific traits or skills. Usually yields a partial or reduced version of the original. Repeated claims of the same type feed into the existing trait rather than granting duplicates. Failed claims yield partial attribute growth. The creature must possess a System-recognized skill or trait — biological features may or may not qualify.

[[Predator's Mandate]] scans for both a skill and a trait simultaneously (two channels). Directed claims carry no additional cost. If no skill exists (common for creatures and most monsters), the skill channel sits empty.

### Mass-Consumption Extraction

When individual sources are too small for full extraction, a progress bar appears. Progress accumulates across many kills of the same trait type. Royal guard-tier creatures provide dramatically more progress per unit than common workers or soldiers. The trait is acquired when progress reaches 100%.

### Hide Status

[[Hide Status]] can hide or blank information. It cannot lie or replace data with false information. The System refuses false input.

### Mana Well

[[Mana Well]] fills from ambient mana. Fill rate depends on environmental mana density. In [[The Deeproot]] at Level 7: approximately 8 seconds from empty to full. Outside the Deeproot the fill rate would be dramatically slower.

### Unaligned Skill Points

Some skill points are unaligned — not tied to the class framework. Unaligned points offer general or structural options (e.g. [[Mana Well]], physical upgrades) rather than class-specific ones.
